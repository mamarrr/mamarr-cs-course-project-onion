# User Personas Specification

## Primary User Groups

The current project structure indicates a product designed for multiple user groups that collaborate around property maintenance and account access.

### Management company users

Management company users are the primary operational users of the platform. They are responsible for overseeing managed portfolios, maintaining business relationships, coordinating maintenance activity, and progressing work through the ticket lifecycle.

Representative personas include:

- company owners or administrators
- managers
- support specialists
- finance-oriented staff

These users need broad visibility within their own company scope, while remaining isolated from other companies in the platform.

### Customer representatives

Customer-side users represent organizations that own, occupy, or otherwise oversee managed properties. They need visibility into relevant properties, tickets, updates, and account relationships without requiring management-level control of the broader platform.

Representative personas include:

- primary representatives
- technical representatives
- billing representatives

### Residents

Residents are end users with a simpler operational focus. Their likely needs include viewing their own unit context, submitting maintenance concerns, following status progress, and responding when additional details or confirmation are required.

### System administrators

System administrators operate at the platform level rather than at a single management company level. Their role is to maintain platform-wide administration, configuration, and support capabilities.

## Persona Expectations

At a high level, the product should provide:

- clear workspace context for each user
- role-appropriate navigation and visibility
- limited access to only the data each persona should see
- workflows tailored to operational depth, from simple resident actions to full company administration

## Collaboration Model

The platform is designed as a shared environment where multiple personas contribute to the same maintenance lifecycle from different perspectives. Management users coordinate work, customer representatives monitor and interact with relevant records, residents surface issues and confirm outcomes, and system administrators maintain the platform foundation.

## Iteration Note

These personas are a high-level product framing based on current repository evidence. They should later be expanded into clearer user journeys, permissions summaries, and role-specific goals.
